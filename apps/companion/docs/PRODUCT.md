# OmniaCompanion Product Definition

Status: planned core product, pre-implementation

## One-line definition

OmniaCompanion is a consumer-facing product for creating, discovering, and building ongoing relationships with AI companions and characters.

## Product promise

The product should make it easy for a user to:

- create or configure a companion with a clear identity
- talk to that companion in a natural, persistent way
- keep a sense of continuity across sessions
- discover other companion concepts without losing personal ownership of their own space

## Primary users

- users who want a personal AI companion experience
- users who enjoy character-driven chat and roleplay
- users who want to create, tune, and revisit custom personas
- future creators who may publish or monetize companions after the core experience is stable

## Core value proposition

OmniaCompanion is not just "another chat UI." Its value comes from:

- character identity
- emotional continuity
- personalization over time
- a clear separation between private companionship and public discovery

## Core user loops

### 1. Create

The user creates a companion by defining name, tone, role, personality, backstory, boundaries, and visual identity.

### 2. Connect

The user chats with the companion and expects consistent tone, memory, and behavior over time.

### 3. Refine

The user adjusts traits, rules, memory settings, and presentation without rebuilding everything from scratch.

### 4. Discover

The user explores featured or community-created companions, then saves, duplicates, or adapts them into their own space.

### 5. Return

The product should reward long-term reuse through continuity, not only novelty.

## Product principles

- Companion first: the emotional and conversational experience is the center of the product.
- Text-first foundation: the MVP must succeed as a text-based companion product before advanced media layers are added.
- Private by default: a user's private companion space must feel separate from public discovery.
- Character consistency matters more than feature count.
- Clear scope beats ecosystem sprawl.

## What OmniaCompanion is

- a dedicated companion product
- a character creation and interaction platform
- a private chat and memory experience with optional discovery layers
- a future home for creator-driven companion publishing after the core user loop is proven

## What OmniaCompanion is not

- not OCOS
- not a monitoring dashboard
- not a generic prompt database
- not a visual generation studio
- not a catch-all shell for unrelated Omnia services
- not dependent on internal control-plane concepts appearing in user experience

## Early product surfaces

- onboarding and identity setup
- companion creation or import
- companion profile and editing
- chat surface
- conversation history
- memory and behavior settings
- private library of saved companions
- lightweight discovery surface

## Monetization stance

Companion can grow into tiered access and creator monetization, but monetization should not distort the MVP. The first release should prove retention and companion quality before marketplace complexity is introduced.

## Success criteria for the first real version

- users can create at least one meaningful companion end to end
- conversation quality feels stable enough to revisit
- the product has a clear repeat-use loop
- the product reads as its own experience without requiring users to understand the Omnia ecosystem
